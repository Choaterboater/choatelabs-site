About

Choate Labs is one person. The plural is aspirational.

I’m Stephen Choate. I’ve spent my career around enterprise networks, which explains most of the catalog: a faster way to identify MAC addresses, a terminal that takes Aruba gear seriously, a way to document a rack without a clipboard and a camera roll full of mystery photos. Spend enough years around switches and you collect a list of small daily irritations that software could fix, so I fix them.

A few rules I try to hold myself to. An app shouldn’t need a tutorial. Data stays on your device unless there’s a very good reason for it to leave. Shipping something imperfect beats polishing something forever.

The game is the exception to every pattern on this site. LUMINA exists because I wanted to know whether I could make something genuinely fun with a browser canvas. The answer, after six skins and a global leaderboard, is apparently yes.

Everything here is designed, built, and maintained by me alone. If something breaks, the email below reaches me directly.
