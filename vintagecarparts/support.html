Frequently asked questions

How does identification work?

Two layers. The live scanner runs entirely on your iPhone — Apple's Vision framework turns what the camera sees into a feature print and matches it against the bundled 216-part catalog, while on-device OCR picks casting numbers off the part. For rare or rough parts, Expert Scan sends the photo you choose to an AI service for a deeper read: identification, era, applications, and condition notes.

Does it work offline?

Mostly, yes. Catalog browsing, the live scanner, casting-number lookup, your garage, and scan history all work with no connection. Three things need internet: Expert Scan (AI identification), VIN decode when adding a vehicle, and the sold-prices link (it opens eBay).

What casting numbers does it understand?

The big three American formats:

  • Ford — e.g. C6AE-6015-A (decade letter, year digit, line, part, revision)
  • GM — 7-digit castings like 3927184 or 7028207
  • Mopar — 7-digit castings from the same era

The scanner reads them off the part automatically, and there's a manual lookup with a live "this looks like a Ford engine casting" hint as you type.

What's in the catalog?

216 vintage parts across eleven systems — carburetion, ignition, engine, cooling, fuel, exhaust, brakes, suspension, steering, electrical, transmission, and body/interior — centered on the American muscle era, with classic European and Japanese entries (Solex, Hitachi, Bosch CIS, and friends). Every part has a studio reference image, specs, and applications. It grows with each release.

How do fitment verdicts work?

Add your cars in the Garage — type a VIN and the app decodes year/make/model automatically through the free public NHTSA database, or enter it manually for pre-1981 vehicles. When a part is identified, its application list is checked against your saved cars and you get a verdict on the spot.

Where do the prices come from?

The "See sold prices" button opens eBay's completed-and-sold listings for the identified part — real transaction prices, not asking prices. The app doesn't set or estimate values itself; it shows you the market.

The identification looks wrong.

It happens — sixty-year-old parts are hard, even for the AI. Try these:

  • Get the casting number in frame — it's the single strongest signal.
  • Wipe the part down; raised numbers under grease read poorly.
  • Shoot in good light, fill the frame with the part.
  • Run an Expert Scan for the deeper AI read.

Still wrong? Email a screenshot and what the part actually is — real corrections directly improve the catalog.

Do I need an account or API key?

No account, ever. Expert Scan works out of the box; if you prefer to run scans on your own OpenRouter API key, you can paste one in Settings and it's stored in the iOS Keychain on your device.

I have a feature request.

Yes please. The roadmap is informal and friendly to suggestions from real users. Email stephen.choate@choatelabs.com with what you'd like and how you'd use it — "add the parts for my car" requests are especially welcome.

Something crashed.

Email stephen.choate@choatelabs.com with your iOS version, device model, and what you were doing when it happened. Screenshots or a screen recording make a real difference.

What does the app collect about me?

Almost nothing — no accounts, no analytics, no tracking. Live scanning is on-device; an Expert Scan sends only the photo you chose. See the privacy policy for the full breakdown.
