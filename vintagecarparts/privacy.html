VintageCarParts Privacy Policy

An iOS app by ChoateLabs

Last updated: June 3, 2026

VintageCarParts ("the app") is built by ChoateLabs. This policy explains, in plain English, what the app does with your data. The short version: almost everything stays on your device, and the one thing that doesn't — a photo you explicitly choose to analyze with Expert Scan — is sent only to produce your identification and nothing else.

What the app does

VintageCarParts helps you identify vintage and classic car parts by pointing your iPhone camera at them, look up casting numbers, browse a built-in parts catalog, save a list of your vehicles to check whether a scanned part is compatible, and jump to sold-price listings for a part.

Data we collect

We do not run our own user accounts, servers, or analytics for this app. The only data the app touches falls into these categories:

1. Live camera feed (on-device only)

When you use the scanner, your live camera feed is processed entirely on your iPhone using Apple's Vision framework. Frames are turned into a numerical "feature print" and matched against a local part database that ships inside the app, and on-device OCR reads casting numbers off the part. The live camera feed is never uploaded anywhere, and we run no servers that receive your images. The one exception — a photo you explicitly submit for AI identification — is described in the next section.

2. Photos you choose to analyze with Expert Scan (sent to an AI provider)

If you run an Expert Scan (the deeper AI identification), the photo you selected for that scan is sent over HTTPS from your phone directly to the AI service, OpenRouter, to produce the identification. This only happens when you initiate it.

  • Only the part photo and the text of the identification request are sent — never your name, Apple ID, email, location, or device identifiers.
  • OpenRouter's handling of submitted content is governed by its own policy at openrouter.ai/privacy.
  • If you supply your own OpenRouter API key in Settings, it is stored in the iOS Keychain on your device and used only to authenticate your own scans.
  • Don't include anything in the photo you wouldn't want a third-party service to process — crop to the part.

3. Scan history (on-device only)

After a scan, the captured image and its result are saved locally:

  • The image file is written to the app's Documents/ScanImages/ directory.
  • The result metadata (part name, confidence, timestamp) is stored in UserDefaults on the device.

Scan history is never uploaded. Deleting the app removes it. You can clear individual scans from the History tab at any time.

4. Vehicle profiles you add manually (on-device, with optional VIN lookup)

If you add a vehicle by VIN, the app sends only the 17-character VIN to the U.S. National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) public vPIC API at https://vpic.nhtsa.dot.gov to translate it into year/make/model. This is a free, public, government-operated lookup — no API key, no account.

  • The NHTSA service is governed by NHTSA's own privacy policy at nhtsa.gov/privacy-policy.
  • We do not send your name, your Apple ID, your location, or anything else alongside the VIN.
  • The decoded result is cached in UserDefaults on your device so we don't re-query the same VIN.

If you skip VIN entry, no network call is made.

5. Settings and filters (on-device only)

Era filters, your confidence threshold, and which vehicle is your "primary" are stored in UserDefaults. An OpenRouter API key you enter yourself is stored in the iOS Keychain. None of it leaves the device.

Third-party services

OpenRouter — AI identification (active, only when you run an Expert Scan)

Described in section 2 above. Used solely to identify the part in the photo you submit; the request carries no account or identity information about you.

NHTSA vPIC API (active, only when you look up a VIN)

Used only when you choose to look up a VIN. See above. We do not control NHTSA's logging — assume any web request to a U.S. government endpoint may be logged on their side per their policy.

Replicate — reference illustrations (disabled in this build)

The app contains an optional feature that generates a studio-style illustration of a part using Replicate's image-generation API. In the version you have installed this feature is disabled. If a future build enables it: only a short text description of the part (e.g. "Holley 4150 carburetor, studio product photography") is sent — never your photos. Replicate's practices are described at replicate.com/privacy.

Firebase (not enabled in this build)

The app contains scaffolding for an optional community feature backed by Google Firebase (Authentication + Firestore). In the version you have installed, Firebase is disabled and all community data shown in the UI is local mock data. No Firebase SDK calls are made.

If a future build enables Firebase:

  • A new account would be created via Firebase Anonymous Authentication. This generates a random user ID; we never receive your email, name, or Apple ID unless you explicitly choose Sign in with Apple in a later release.
  • Contributions you choose to publish (photos you submit to the community, text corrections, comments) would be stored in Cloud Firestore under that anonymous ID.
  • Firebase's own privacy practices apply: firebase.google.com/support/privacy.
  • Anything you publish to the community is, by definition, public. Don't upload photos you don't want others to see.

We will update this policy and the in-app About section before turning Firebase on for real.

Outbound links (eBay / Amazon / RockAuto)

The app shows links for sourcing and pricing parts — for example, "See sold prices" opens an eBay search for completed listings. Tapping any of these opens the partner's site in your browser; the app itself sends them nothing beyond the link you tapped, and once there the partner's own privacy policy applies.

What we do NOT collect

  • We do not collect your location.
  • We do not collect contacts, calendars, photos library, or microphone.
  • We do not use third-party analytics, ad networks, or crash reporters in this build.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share data with marketers. We have no data to sell.

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect information from them.

Your rights and controls

Because nearly all data lives on your device, you control it directly:

  • Delete a scan: swipe to delete in the History tab.
  • Remove a vehicle: swipe to delete in Settings → My Vehicles.
  • Remove your API key: clear it in Settings; it is deleted from the Keychain.
  • Clear the VIN cache: delete and reinstall the app.
  • Revoke camera access: iOS Settings → VintageCarParts → Camera.
  • Erase everything: delete the app from your iPhone.

Photos submitted to OpenRouter are processed to produce your identification; retention on their side is governed by their policy linked above. If a future version stores data with us (e.g., community contributions via Firebase), you will be able to request deletion by emailing the address below, and we will honor applicable requests under GDPR, CCPA, and similar laws.

Security

Local data is protected by iOS's standard app sandbox and, when your device is locked with a passcode, by iOS data protection. API keys you enter live in the iOS Keychain. All network calls — VIN lookup and Expert Scan — use HTTPS.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we'll update the "Last updated" date at the top and, when relevant, surface a notice in the app. Continued use after an update means you accept the revised policy.
