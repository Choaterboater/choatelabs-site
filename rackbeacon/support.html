Frequently asked questions

How do I create my first rack?

Tap the Racks tab, then the + button. Give the rack a name and pick a U size (4U through 52U). Tap any empty U slot to add a device — you can pick from the bundled library or create a custom entry. Devices snap to their actual U height; the faceplate renders automatically.

Does the camera scanner work offline?

Yes. OCR runs entirely on-device through Apple's Vision framework and DataScannerViewController. There is no network code in the app at all. It works on a plane, in a basement, or any closet that has lost its uplink.

What devices are pre-loaded in the library?

Out of the box, RackBeacon ships models for:

  • Aruba CX 6000 / 8000 / 9000 / 10000 series
  • Cisco Catalyst 9k, Nexus, ISR, ASR, Firepower, UCS
  • Juniper EX, QFX, SRX, MX
  • Fortinet FortiGate and FortiSwitch
  • Palo Alto Networks PA series
  • HPE ProLiant servers
  • APC Smart-UPS

Each entry includes U size and port layout so the faceplate renders correctly. If something you use isn't in the library, you can add a custom device — and email a request to get it added to the next bundled release.

What devices do I need for room capture?

Floor-plan capture uses Apple RoomPlan, which requires a LiDAR sensor. That means an iPhone Pro 12 or newer, or an iPad Pro 2020 or newer. This is a hardware limitation of Apple RoomPlan, not the app.

Everything else — racks, devices, the camera scanner, exports — works on any iPhone or iPad running iOS 18+.

Can I export to NetBox?

Yes. Use Export → CSV to get a NetBox-compatible CSV — one rack per file. The columns line up with NetBox's device-import schema. Full-site JSON export is also available, and floor plans export as SVG.

Will my data sync across devices?

Not in v1.0. Each device's data lives in its own RackBeacon Documents folder. iCloud sync is on the roadmap for v1.1. In the meantime, the JSON export is a clean way to move a project from iPhone to iPad or to back it up.

The camera scan didn't read my label.

Try these in order:

  • Tap the label inside the reticle to focus.
  • Turn on the torch if the label is dim or chrome-finished.
  • Get closer — most equipment labels are tiny. The camera prefers about 4–8 inches.
  • Make sure the field you want (serial, MAC, IMEI) is fully visible inside the frame.
  • For a stubborn label, take a still photo and let the OCR run on the captured image instead.

If OCR consistently fails on a label that's clearly readable to you, email a photo and we'll improve it.

I have a feature request.

Yes please. The roadmap is informal and friendly to suggestions from real users. Email stephen.choate@choatelabs.com with what you'd like and how you'd use it.

Something crashed.

Email stephen.choate@choatelabs.com with your iOS version, device model, and what you were doing when it happened. Screenshots or a screen recording make a real difference.

What about MAC Vendor Lookup integration?

RackBeacon doesn't currently look up MAC vendors via Shortcuts — that integration with the sibling app, MAC Vendor Lookup, is planned for v1.1. For now, copy a MAC out of RackBeacon and paste it into MAC Vendor Lookup when you need the OUI.
