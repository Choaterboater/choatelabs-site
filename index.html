Choate Labs

This is the workshop of Stephen Choate. I’ve spent my career around enterprise networks, and most of what’s here began as a tool I needed on the job: a faster way to identify MAC addresses, a terminal that takes Aruba gear seriously, a way to document a rack without a clipboard and a camera roll full of mystery photos.

Some of these grew into real products on the App Store. One turned into a game. Everything here is built and maintained by one person, and the email at the bottom of the page reaches me directly.

Projects

01

GreenCli

macOS & Windows · v1.0 out now

A desktop terminal for people who run Aruba and HPE networks. SSH, Telnet, and serial in one window, syntax highlighting that knows more than one vendor, an encrypted credential vault, and an AI assistant for the days the exact command escapes you. Signed and notarized builds for macOS and Windows.

Download GreenCli

02

LUMINA: Neon Orbit

web & iOS · playable now

A one-touch arcade game. You orbit, you flip direction, you try not to die. It started as a weekend canvas experiment and somehow ended up with six skins, a global leaderboard, and an App Store release. No install needed, it runs in the browser.

Play LUMINA

03

MAC Vendor Lookup

iOS · on the App Store

Type, paste, or scan a MAC address and get the manufacturer. The full IEEE OUI database lives on the phone, so it works in a basement with no signal, which is exactly where you tend to need it. Built because I was tired of pasting OUIs into a search engine.

About the app

04

VoltaNode

web · running

A paper-trading platform where strategy bots trade against live markets through Alpaca. EMA, MACD, mean reversion, and news sentiment, with an LLM advisor that pulls fundamentals, technicals, and macro into a single thesis. Paper money only. The lessons are real, the losses are not.

How it works

05

RackBeacon

iOS · in development

Documentation for racks, closets, and comms rooms. Scan device labels, place gear in a rack diagram, capture the room with LiDAR. Everything stays on the device.

Project page

06

VintageCarParts

iOS · in development

Point the camera at an old car part and find out what it is, what it fits, and roughly what it’s worth. OCR for casting numbers, a 216-part reference catalog, and real eBay sold prices instead of wishful asking prices.

Project page

07

ChoateLab

self-hosted · in development

A dashboard for the home lab. Docker, Plex, Pi-hole, Home Assistant, the *arr stack, and whatever else you run, arranged on one drag-and-drop home screen. Open source.
